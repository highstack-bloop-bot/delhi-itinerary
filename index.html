Day 1 · Friday 26 June

New Delhi & the Lutyens Heart

Imperial avenues, a perfect Mughal tomb, and leafy garden tombs — all close to your hotel.
8:30 AM

India Gate & Kartavya Path

📍 Rajpath · ~7 km from hotel

Begin at the 42 m war memorial arch and stroll the freshly redeveloped Kartavya Path toward the floodlit canopy. Early morning means soft light, joggers and far fewer crowds. Glimpse the President's Estate (Rashtrapati Bhavan) and Parliament at the far end.

Go early — shade is scarceFree30–45 min
10:30 AM

Humayun's Tomb

📍 Nizamuddin East · UNESCO Site

The garden-tomb that rehearsed the Taj Mahal — red sandstone and white marble set in formal charbagh gardens. Quieter than the Taj and arguably more elegant. Wander to the nearby Isa Khan's octagonal tomb inside the same complex.

₹600 foreigner / ₹40 Indian1.5 hrsBest photos: morning
1:30 PM

Lunch at Khan Market → Lodhi Garden

📍 Khan Market / Lodhi Road

Lunch among Delhi's smartest cafés at Khan Market, then escape the heat in the 90-acre Lodhi Garden — 15th-century Sayyid & Lodhi-era tombs scattered across lawns and old trees. Pair it with the open-air Lodhi Art District murals nearby if you have energy.

Garden freeShade & benchesSunset spot
7:00 PM

Evening at Connaught Place

📍 CP · the colonnaded city centre

Round off with dinner at colonial-era Connaught Place — choose legendary butter chicken at Kake Da Hotel, refined regional food at Saravana Bhavan (veg), or rooftop cocktails. Don't miss Wenger's for pastries if open.

Day 2 · Saturday 27 June

Old Delhi & the Mughal City

Ramparts, the grandest mosque in India, and the most delicious chaos of Chandni Chowk.
8:30 AM

Red Fort (Lal Qila)

📍 Netaji Subhash Marg · UNESCO Site

Shah Jahan's mighty red-sandstone fort and seat of Mughal power for 200 years. Enter at Lahori Gate through the covered Chatta Chowk bazaar to the Diwan-i-Aam and marble Diwan-i-Khas. Go at opening to beat heat and crowds.

Closed Mondays (you're fine)₹600 / ₹351.5 hrs
10:45 AM

Jama Masjid

📍 5 min from Red Fort

India's largest mosque, with a courtyard for 25,000 worshippers. Climb the southern minaret for a sweeping rooftop view over Old Delhi's tangle of lanes. Dress modestly (robes provided); avoid prayer times.

Entry free · camera feeCover shoulders/kneesRemove shoes
12:00 PM

Chandni Chowk & Khari Baoli

📍 Old Delhi's main bazaar

Dive into the 17th-century market — a cycle-rickshaw ride is the classic way through the crush. Eat your way down Paranthe Wali Gali, sample kebabs at Karim's near Jama Masjid, then walk Asia's largest spice market, Khari Baoli (the chillies will make you sneeze).

Cash handyStreet-food heavenTake a rickshaw
5:00 PM

Raj Ghat & a slow evening

📍 Ring Road, by the Yamuna

Pause at the serene black-marble memorial to Mahatma Gandhi, set in landscaped lawns — a calm counterpoint to the morning's intensity. Head back to refresh, then dinner of slow-cooked Mughlai food (try Bukhara at ITC Maurya if you want the city's most famous dal & kebabs — book ahead).

FreeQuiet & green
Day 3 · Sunday 28 June

South Delhi, Towers & Temples

A soaring victory tower, a lotus of marble, the dazzling Akshardham, and a buzzing village of cafés.
8:30 AM

Qutub Minar Complex

📍 Mehrauli · UNESCO Site

The 73 m soaring 12th-century victory tower — the tallest brick minaret in the world — amid a complex of early Indo-Islamic ruins, the Alai Darwaza, and the famous rust-resistant Iron Pillar. Go first thing while the stone is cool.

₹600 / ₹401 hr
10:30 AM

Lotus Temple (Bahá'í House of Worship)

📍 Kalkaji · 27 marble petals

An architectural icon shaped like a half-open lotus — all are welcome to sit in silence inside, regardless of faith. Beautiful gardens and pools surround it. Note: closed Mondays, but Sunday works perfectly.

FreeSilence insideShoes off
2:00 PM

Swaminarayan Akshardham

📍 Noida Mor · vast & ornate

A breathtaking modern temple of intricately carved sandstone and marble, with exhibitions, a step-well courtyard and a musical fountain show after dark. Allow plenty of time. No phones/cameras/bags inside — free cloakroom provided. Closed Mondays.

Entry freeSecurity is strictEvening fountain show
6:00 PM

Hauz Khas Village + Dilli Haat

📍 South Delhi · ruins meet nightlife

End with the medieval reservoir, ruined madrasa and tomb of Hauz Khas overlooking a deer park — then the village's boutiques, rooftop bars and restaurants for a lively final evening. Prefer crafts? Swing by Dilli Haat for handicrafts and regional food stalls.

Eat Like a Local

What to Eat in Delhi

Savoury & street

Butter Chicken

Delhi-born classic

Tandoori chicken in a silky tomato-butter gravy — invented in Delhi at Moti Mahal. Best mopped up with naan.

Chole Bhature

Punjabi breakfast

Spicy chickpea curry with giant puffed fried bread. Try it at Sita Ram Diwan Chand in Paharganj.

Stuffed Parathas

Paranthe Wali Gali

Ghee-fried flatbreads stuffed with potato, paneer, even cashew — a 100-year-old Chandni Chowk lane is devoted to them.

Kebabs at Karim's

Old Delhi institution

Mughlai seekh and shami kebabs, mutton korma and rumali roti, served since 1913 beside Jama Masjid.

Golgappa / Chaat

Street snack

Crisp hollow shells filled with spiced water — plus aloo tikki and dahi bhalla. UPSC/CP stalls are famous.

Biryani & Korma

Mughlai feast

Fragrant layered rice with slow-cooked meat. Old Delhi's Matia Mahal lane is the place to hunt it down.

Sweet endings

Jalebi

Hot & syrupy

Coils of deep-fried batter soaked in saffron syrup. Old Famous Jalebi Wala in Chandni Chowk is the address.

Kulfi Falooda

Beat the heat

Dense, slow-churned Indian ice cream with vermicelli and rose. Kuremal Mohan Lal serves stuffed-fruit kulfi.

Daulat ki Chaat

Winter-only froth

An ethereal saffron milk-foam dessert of Old Delhi — seasonal, so a summer visit may miss it, but ask around.
